[*Back*](./index.html) # Discovery ## Optimal Pipeline We apply our methodology of discriminability to fMRI processing. In particular, we processed 12 test-retest fMRI data sets using 64 processing pipelines. We then compute discriminability estimates of processed data sets. Based on the results, we claim CC200 is a better atlas compared to HOX, AAL and DES. In the meantime, we find that global signal regression and NFF significantly improve discriminability. No scrubbing and FSL is slightly better than scrubbing and ANTS. Furthermore, we discovered that using rank graphs is more discriminable than raw correlation graphs. We are going to further investigate processing pipeline for DTI data sets and compare discriminability of DTI and fMRI.
